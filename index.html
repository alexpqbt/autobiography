getting to know me - dropping lore like a flavor text

Hello! My full name is Alexandre T. Paquibot. I was 20 years old when I was writing this. I was born in Lapu-Lapu City, and I still live in Lapu-Lapu City. I am the youngest in my family of four: my father, mother, sister, and me. I am always the weird one in my family. I talk to myself out loud even if they can listen to me. Sometimes I randomly sing, laugh, or yell unexpectedly. Maybe it's my way of relieving stress, but I'm not so sure about that. Aside from my weirdness, I have a different physique and am unusually tall compared to them, but the only thing that I have in common physically with them is the graying of hair and poor eyesight.

I am currently a college student at the University of Cebu in Lapu-Lapu and Mandaue, pursuing the Bachelor's Science in Information Technology, but let me be frank, I was disappointed that they removed the Computer Science program since that's what I wanted, but alas, it was notorious for being so difficult that the number of students enrolled in that program is so small that they dissolved it for this year. The majors I'm taking as I was writing were 'easy' to me, and I am thankful for it because I have more time to focus on the minor subjects, but this moment will change in the next semester, and I am somewhat excited about them since I'm going to be learning the 'big boy' stuff but worried that I have no time to focus on the other subjects. Oh, and as a side note, I am also a working scholar at UC Medical.

So, how do I spend my free time? Like any veteran shut-in introvert, I browse through the internet for something mildly entertaining, play video games if I feel like it, read books or web novels, listen to any music that makes me say, "Music is like music to my ears," practice playing guitar (I still suck at it) , lie in my bed, daydreaming and occasionally reflecting at myself, want to know why I believe this, why I do this, why I behave like that, and so on. Your typical introverted life. As far as I can remember, coding and mathematics are the only things that I'm passionate about. Things that push my small brain to its limits stimulates me, and writing this sentence made me think that maybe I am an adrenaline junkie.

To describe my personality, my "personne," if you will, would be biased toward me, so I decided to ask my friend. He said that I am introverted but not shy, don't respect social norms, get hyperfixed on something, and dig out everything it has until I eventually burn out. To expand on the last part, I am currently procrastinating on hyperfixation since I am not that bored yet, and speaking of boredom, boredom is the final factor to press the accelerator of my interest and curiosity. Boredom has led me to unexpected things, like programming, cybersecurity, relearning the fundamentals of mathematics, guitar, non-fictional books, unorthodox music, etc. To describe my "personne," in my own biased opinion, it would be perfectly summed up by the Oxford Dictionary: "a person with a compulsive desire for excitement and adventure." I am always looking for things that would appeal to my inner child; living in the moment and not worrying about the far future helped me endure the cold, uncaring life a little bit.

Once I begin to think about the words like "personne", I started thinking about what's my philosophy in life, so let's talk about that. Some of my guiding philosophies in life recently are the phrase "It is what it is" and "Carpe diem". "It is what it is" reflects a mindset that is related to certain philosophical concepts, such as existentialism and stoicism. Existentialism is a philosophy that emphasizes individual existence, freedom, and the responsibility of individuals to create meaning and purpose in their own lives. Stoicism is a philosophy of maintaining inner tranquility and accepting the things that are beyond our control. It emphasizes the development of self-discipline, virtue, and living in accordance with nature. While not a formal philosophy itself, the phrase captures a pragmatic approach to accepting circumstances as they are and focusing on our response to them rather than resisting or dwelling on them. "Carpe diem", which is a Latin for "seize the day". While this phrase is a reword for something more 'memorable', the phrase convey a resilient mindset in the face of setbacks or failures. It implies a willingness to bounce back, learn from experiences, and keep moving forward despite obstacles or disappointments.

"Realization clicks like a puzzle piece falling into perfect alignment," a quote that I have made at the top of my mind when I was about to answer the question of "How do you approach learning and personal growth?" My learning has always been spontaneous and informal, like if I see something interesting or something in the part of my life that I am curious about, I just find a book or any resources that relates to it and read it. For example, it was summer and I was laying in bed like usual, but I was bored out of my mind, and I thought about how college is right around the corner, so I decided to find a book that brushes off the dust of my understanding of mathematics and just do it. The book was called "Everything You Need to Ace Math in One Big Fat Notebook: The Complete Middle School Study Guide" by Altair Peterson, and the most memorable thing about reading that book is that when I finally reached the last page, I genuinely smiled. I think I smiled because I genuinely enjoyed that book and was proud of myself that I finished it. I still remember that feeling to this day, and this feeling made me pursue reading more books. I am always striving to become the better version of yesterday; sometimes I fail to do so, and sometimes I succeed, and I am okay with that, failing to reach my short goal, sometimes the goal of becoming a better version of myself.

In terms of my favorite books, movies, or pieces of art, my literary preferences lean towards the unconventional and informative. I find joy in exploring diverse topics, much like my spontaneous approach to learning mentioned earlier. Among my favorite books are "Sapiens: A Brief History of Humankind," a thought-provoking exploration of the human journey through history, and "Jackal Among Snakes," a gripping narrative that delves into complex themes. Additionally, "Assassin's Apprentice" and "The Blade Itself" have secured spots on my list, each contributing to my appreciation for immersive and well-crafted fantasy worlds. In the realm of movies and art, my tastes remain eclectic, favoring the unorthodox and thought-provoking in both visual and narrative mediums.

I aim to evolve into a more resilient, adaptable, and well-rounded individual, not only in my academic pursuits but also in my personal and professional endeavors. Reflecting on achievements, one that stands out is my rekindled passion for mathematics and perseverance through a challenging programming course through self-directed learning. The completion of the mathematics study guide mentioned earlier marked a significant accomplishment for me, evoking genuine pride and a sense of personal growth. Embracing the challenges posed by academia and my own quirks, I navigate the maze of unconventional thoughts and spontaneous pursuits with the understanding that each step contributes to my mosaic of achievements and failures, shaping the unique tapestry of my journey through life. the big 5 - in the flavor of the power rangers

Sadness, moodiness, and emotional instability are characteristics of neuroticism. Individuals with high neuroticism are prone to mood fluctuations, anxiety, impatience, and unhappiness. Those with low levels of this personality trait are more stable and emotionally resilient.

Extraversion is a personality attribute that is characterized by excitability, friendliness, talkativeness, assertiveness, and a high level of emotional expressiveness. Extraverts are gregarious and tend to gain energy in social situations. Being in the company of others makes them feel invigorated and excited. People that are introverted or have a low level of this personality trait are more reserved. They have less energy to expend in social situations, and social gatherings can be exhausting. Introverts frequently require alone and silence in order to "recharge."

Openness stresses imagination and insight the most. People with a high level of openness tend to have a diverse set of interests. They are fascinated by the world and other people, and they are ready to learn new things and have new experiences. People with a high level of this personality trait are also more daring and innovative. People that score low on this personality feature tend to be more traditional and may struggle with abstract thinking.

Trust, altruism, friendliness, affection, and other prosocial acts are examples of agreeableness. People with high agreeableness tend to be more cooperative, whereas those with low agreeableness tend to be more competitive, and sometimes even manipulative.

Conscientiousness is characterized by forethought, good impulse control , and goal-directed behavior. Highly conscientious people are structured and detail-oriented. They plan ahead of time, consider how their actions influence others, and keep deadlines in mind. Someone with a lower score on this key personality attribute is less ordered and orderly. They may postpone in order to finish tasks, sometimes entirely missing deadlines.
